Traditional Recruiting Traps vs. Intelligent Discovery

⚠️ The Traditional Keyword Trap

Conventional Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) rely on simple keyword counting, making them highly vulnerable to:

  • Keyword Stuffers: Non-technical candidates who paste high-frequency buzzwords into descriptions to cheat search embeddings.
  • Research-Only Profiles: Finding candidates with matching keywords (e.g., CV/Speech papers) who have zero production or IR infrastructure experience.
  • Silent Candidates: Perfect-on-paper profiles who are entirely inactive, unavailable, or have extremely low recruiter reply rates.

💡 Our Intelligent Discovery Philosophy

We designed a multi-dimensional matching engine that aligns with the judgment of a world-class recruiter:

  • Semantic Match & Experience Curve: Evaluates context rather than occurrences, verifying experience duration and role trajectories.
  • Anti-Service Exclusions: Automatically filters candidates whose entire career is in pure consulting/service firms (TCS, Infosys, etc.).
  • Behavioral Multiplier: Treats candidate availability, notice periods, and active platform engagement as key ranking modifiers.

The Honeypot Shield: Disqualification Defense

Security Goal

Identify and block impossible candidate profiles ("honeypots") to maintain 0% trap presence in our top ranks (disqualification occurs if honeypots > 10% in top 100).

The dataset embeds synthetic trap profiles with logical contradictions. Our ranking code executes a robust pre-filter pass that checks every record for temporal and profile anomalies, forcing their scores to 0.0 immediately.

Rule 1: Startup Founding Dates

Detects candidates claiming to have started working at Indian AI startups before they were legally founded:
• Krutrim (founded April 2023)
• Sarvam AI (founded July 2023)
Result: Catches 87 impossible profiles.

Rule 2: Career Duration Mismatch

Compares declared career job durations against start and end dates.
• e.g. Start: Sept 2023, End: None (Max possible: 33 months), but declared duration is 166 months.
Result: Catches 19 duration-mismatch honeypots.

Engine Architecture: 5-Pillar Fit Score

Pillar Weight Logic and Matching Mechanism
Experience Curve 15% Non-linear fit curve. Peaks at ideal 5–9 years. Penalizes junior candidates (<4 years) and slightly discounts over-senior managers (>12 years).
Applied ML Role Fit 25% Measures career duration in applied AI/ML titles. Scans job descriptions for key engineering/retrieval concepts. Heavily penalizes candidates in unrelated domains (marketing, HR).
Pedigree & Exclusions 20% IT Services exclusion rule. If 100% of candidate's career is at large consulting firms (TCS, Wipro, Infosys, etc.), they receive a pedigree penalty. Rewards product-company backgrounds.
Required Skills 25% Scans for required categories: Vector DBs (Milvus, Pinecone, FAISS), Embeddings (sentence-transformers), Python, and Eval (NDCG, MAP). Factored with endorsements & duration.
Semantic Similarity 15% Fits a local TF-IDF model on profile summaries and work details, calculating cosine similarity against core JD concepts (dense retrieval, evaluation, search scale).

The Behavioral Multiplier: Real Availability

Notice Period (Stated)

A candidate with 90-day notice is often too slow to hire.

• Notice ≤ 30 days: Multiplier = 1.0
• Notice ≤ 60 days: Multiplier = 0.9
• Notice ≥ 90 days: Multiplier = 0.35

Location & Relocate

Primary offices are in Noida and Pune.

• In Pune / Noida / NCR: Multiplier = 1.0
• Willing to Relocate: Multiplier = 0.9
• Outside India (No sponsorship): Multiplier = 0.45

Platform Engagement

Filters out candidates who logged off months ago.

• Active ≤ 30 days: Multiplier = 1.0
• Active ≥ 180 days: Multiplier = 0.3
• Response Rate ≥ 70%: Multiplier = 1.0

Formulation: Final Score = Base Fit Score × Notice Factor × Location Factor × Active Factor × OpenToWork Factor × GitHub Factor

Grounded Reasoning Generator

📝 Dynamic Fact-Based Synthesis

Our reasoning generator is fully offline and runs dynamically in milliseconds, adhering to the 6 criteria of the manual evaluation:

  • No Hallucinations: Incorporates variables read directly from the candidate profile (exact companies, years, and skills).
  • JD Alignment: Explicitly cites search database systems (Milvus, Pinecone) or evaluation frameworks (NDCG).
  • Honest Concerns: Notes if notice periods are long, or if the candidate's career has been entirely in service firms.
  • High Variation: Rotates through multiple grammatical structures to prevent robotic templates.
